Volatility-Timing Target-Date Fund

A target-date fund strategy that scales equity exposure inversely with realized volatility, backtested from 1976 through 2024 against a conventional glide path benchmark.

Context

Target-date funds dominate defined-contribution plans, yet their glide paths are largely deterministic: equity weight declines as a fixed function of years to retirement. The path ignores prevailing market conditions. A 2008 retiree riding a static glide path absorbed the full equity drawdown at the worst possible moment in the lifecycle.

The thesis: a small, rules-based deviation from the static glide path, conditioned on realized volatility, can preserve long-run expected return while compressing the left tail.

Approach

Equity exposure scales inversely with a 60-day realized volatility estimate, capped between 50% and 130% of the baseline glide path weight. The bond sleeve absorbs the difference, with risk-parity weighting across duration buckets. Rebalancing runs monthly to limit turnover.

Two design choices mattered. First, the volatility lookback window is short enough to react to regime changes but long enough to avoid noise-driven flips. Second, the cap on deviation from the baseline glide path keeps the strategy fiduciary-compliant; the fund still tracks its target date.

Backtest

Data spans 1976 through 2024, covering Volcker, the dot-com peak and unwind, the global financial crisis, and the 2020-2022 cycle. Returns are net of an estimated 25 bps expense ratio. The benchmark is a representative 2030-vintage TDF glide path.

Recognition

Second place at the Cornell Investment Practitioners Case Competition, 2025. Judges flagged the cap-and-floor structure as the differentiator; most submissions ran unconstrained vol-targeting and surrendered too much upside to be deployable in a fiduciary product.

What I Learned

Drawdown matters more than mean return when the investor cannot time their exit. A retiree near the target date does not get a second crisis to recover from. Constraining the strategy to remain recognizable as a TDF, rather than maximizing the in-sample Sharpe, was the choice that held up under scrutiny.
